How to Play

A secret MLB player has been chosen. Guess players by name and you'll get back a similarity score (0-1000) comparing your guess to the secret player. The higher the score, the more similar the careers. 1000 means you got it.

Scoring

You start with 101 points. Each guess costs 1 point. Each hint costs 20 points. Score floors at 0 but you can keep guessing. A correct answer scores whatever points remain -- so a perfect game is 100.

Hints

You can reveal up to 5 hints at any time, each costing 20 points: primary position, birth year, career WAR, primary team, and country of birth.

Cross-Role Guesses

If the secret player is a pitcher and you guess a position player (or vice versa), you'll get a score of 0. Use that as a signal to switch sides.

Similarity Score Formula

For position players, career stats compared include G, AB, R, H, 2B, 3B, HR, RBI, BB, SO, SB, BA, and SLG. For pitchers: W, L, SV, IP, K, and ERA. Based on the Baseball Reference similarity score methodology.

Player Pool

The pool includes players who had meaningful MLB careers:

  • Position players: 500+ career games
  • Starting pitchers: 100+ career starts, majority of appearances as starter
  • Relief pitchers: 300+ career appearances, majority in relief

The era filter lets you narrow the secret player's era by the years their career overlapped.

Daily vs. Unlimited

Daily mode picks the same player for everyone on a given day (like Wordle). Unlimited mode picks a new random player each game.
