How to Play

A secret MLB player has been chosen. Guess players by name and you'll get back a similarity score (0-1000) comparing your guess to the secret player. The higher the score, the more similar the careers. 1000 means you got it.

Scoring

You start with 101 points. Each guess costs 1 point. Each hint costs 20 points. Score floors at 0 but you can keep guessing. A correct answer scores whatever points remain, so a perfect game is 100.

Hints

You can reveal up to 5 hints at any time, each costing 20 points: primary position, birth year, career WAR, primary team, and country of birth.

Guess Badges

Each guess shows green or yellow badges when your guessed player is close to the secret player. Green means an exact match (same position or same primary team). Yellow means close (birth year within 5, or WAR within 10).

Similarity Breakdown

Each guess also shows which stat was most similar and which was least similar to the secret player, to help you narrow your search.

Cross-Role Guesses

If the secret player is a pitcher and you guess a position player (or vice versa), you'll get a score of 0. Use that as a signal to switch sides.

Similarity Score Formula

For position players, career stats compared include G, AB, R, H, 2B, 3B, HR, RBI, BB, SO, SB, BA, and SLG. For pitchers: W, L, SV, IP, K, and ERA. Based on the Baseball Reference similarity score methodology. All stats are as of Opening Day 2026.

Player Pool

The pool includes players who had meaningful MLB careers:

  • Position players: 500 or more career games
  • Starting pitchers: 100 or more career starts, with the majority of appearances as a starter
  • Relief pitchers: 300 or more career appearances, with the majority in relief

Year Range

The year range filter controls which players can be chosen as the secret player. A player is included if their estimated career window (age 18 to 45 based on birth year) overlaps with the selected range. You can narrow it to a specific decade or even a single year to focus on a particular era.

Daily vs. Unlimited

Daily mode picks the same player for everyone on a given day (like Wordle). Unlimited mode picks a new random player each game.
