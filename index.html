How to Play

A secret MLB player has been chosen. Guess any player by name and you will receive a similarity score (0–1000) comparing their career stats to the secret player. The higher the score, the more similar the careers. A score of 1000 means you found them.

Scoring

You start with 101 points. Each guess costs 1 point. Score floors at 0 but you can keep guessing. A correct answer scores whatever points remain — a perfect game (first guess, no hints) scores 100.

Hints

Eight hints are available. Costs are based on how hard each hint is to brute-force through guessing. Hints are ordered cheapest to most expensive:

Hard mode: max 2 hints, TOP 3 not available.

Difficulty

  • Easy: all five badges shown per guess, plus Hot/Cold stat indicators
  • Medium: position and birth year badges only, plus Hot/Cold
  • Hard: no badges, no Hot/Cold, max 2 hints

Guess Badges

Each guess shows up to five badges (Easy) or two (Medium) comparing your guess to the secret player. Green means exact or very close. Yellow means close.

  • POS: green if same primary position; yellow if the secret player has played 10+ games there
  • TEAM: green if same franchise; yellow if same division
  • BORN: green if exact birth year; yellow if within 5 years
  • WAR: green if within 2.0; yellow if within 10.0
  • HOME: green if same birth country

Hot / Cold

Available in Easy and Medium. Each guess shows the stat most similar (Hot) and least similar (Cold) to the secret player.

Cross-Role Guesses

Guessing a pitcher when the secret player is a position player (or vice versa) scores 0. Use it as a signal to switch sides.

Similarity Score

The similarity score concept was created by Bill James and later adopted by Baseball Reference. Scores start at 1000 and are reduced based on differences in career stats. All stats as of Opening Day 2026. See the BBRef methodology page for more.

Player Pool

Position players with 500+ career games. Starting pitchers with 100+ starts. Relief pitchers with 300+ appearances. Shohei Ohtani and Babe Ruth each appear twice — once as a pitcher, once as a position player.

Filters

Era filters the secret player pool by career overlap. Team limits the pool to players whose primary team maps to that franchise. Daily mode picks the same player for everyone each day; Unlimited picks randomly. The Negro Lg preset limits the pool to Negro League players.
