How to Play

A secret MLB player has been chosen. Guess any player by name to receive a similarity score (0–1000) comparing their career stats to the secret player. Higher score = more similar careers. A score of 1000 means you found them.

Scoring

You start with 101 points. Each guess costs 1 point. Score floors at 0 but you can keep guessing. A correct answer scores whatever points remain — a perfect game (1 guess, no hints) scores 100.

Difficulty

Difficulty controls how much information each guess reveals. It does not change the player pool — Easy, Medium, and Hard all draw from the same set of players. Hard is not harder players, it is a harder guessing process.

  • Easy: each guess shows 5 badges (position, franchise, birth year, WAR, country) plus Hot/Cold stat indicators. Up to 8 hints available.
  • Medium: each guess shows 2 badges (position and birth year) plus Hot/Cold. Up to 8 hints available at higher cost.
  • Hard: no badges, no Hot/Cold. Maximum 2 hints. TOP 3 hint not available.

Guess Badges (Easy & Medium)

Green = exact match or very close. Yellow = close. Grey = far off.

  • POS: green if same primary position; yellow if secret player has played 10+ games at that position
  • FRANCH: green if same franchise (historical names mapped to current); yellow if same division
  • BORN: green if exact birth year; yellow if within 5 years
  • WAR: green if within 2.0 career WAR; yellow if within 10.0
  • HOME: green if same birth country

Hot / Cold (Easy & Medium)

Each guess shows the single stat most similar to the secret player (Hot, shown in green) and least similar (Cold, shown in red). Stats used depend on whether the player is a hitter or pitcher.

Cross-Role Guesses

If you guess a pitcher and the secret player is a position player (or vice versa), the score will show as 0 and the card will be labeled "Wrong role" to let you know why. Use it as a free signal to switch sides.

Hints

Hints are priced based on how many guesses it would take to brute-force the same information. Cheaper hints give less unique info; expensive hints would take many guesses to figure out on your own.

Similarity Score

Based on the similarity score method created by Bill James and used by Baseball Reference. Scores start at 1000 and are reduced by differences in career stats. Position players are compared on counting stats (G, AB, R, H, 2B, 3B, HR, RBI, BB, SO, SB) and rate stats (BA, OBP, SLG). Pitchers are compared on W, L, SV, IP, K, BB, and ERA. All stats as of Opening Day 2026.

Player Pool

Position players with 500+ career games. Starting pitchers with 100+ career starts (majority as starter). Relief pitchers with 300+ appearances (majority in relief). Shohei Ohtani and Babe Ruth each appear twice — once as a pitcher and once as a position player.

Era Filter

Filters the secret player pool by career overlap — a player is included if any part of their career falls within the selected range. Use presets or enter custom years. The Negro Lg preset limits the pool to players from recognized Negro League franchises (careers 1920–1948).

Franchise Filter

Limits the secret player pool to players whose primary franchise matches the selection. Historical team names are mapped to their current franchise (e.g. Brooklyn Dodgers = Los Angeles Dodgers, Montreal Expos = Washington Nationals).

Daily vs. Unlimited

Daily mode picks the same secret player for everyone on a given day, seeded by date and era. Unlimited picks a new random player each game.

Challenge a Friend

After any game ends, tap Challenge a Friend to pick any player and generate a shareable link. Your friend opens the link and must guess that player.
